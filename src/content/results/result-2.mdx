summary: "For bulk states drawn from the product class, entropy disagreement scales as 0.608 times d_B to the negative one-half power. The prefactor is analytic, not fit to data: it equals sqrt(4(pi^2/3 - 3)/pi). Simulations match to under 1.3 standard deviations at every test point, including out-of-sample."
---

For bulk states of the form $|\psi_A\rangle \otimes |\psi_B\rangle \otimes |\psi_C\rangle$ with each factor Haar-distributed (Theorem 4.2):

$$\mathbb{E}|S_A - S_B| = \sqrt{\frac{4(\pi^2/3 - 3)}{\pi\, d_B}}\,(1 + o(1)) \approx 0.6076 \cdot d_B^{-1/2}$$

The prefactor $\sqrt{4(\pi^2/3-3)/\pi} \approx 0.608$ is exact. It is not fit to data. It comes from the variance of the Shannon entropy of a flat Dirichlet distribution (Lemma 4.1): $d \cdot \mathrm{Var}(H) \to \pi^2/3 - 3 \approx 0.2899$. Simulations matched the prediction to under $1.3\sigma$ at every test point, including the out-of-sample check at $d_B = 20$.
