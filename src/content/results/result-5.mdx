title: "Haar subleading structure"
resultNumber: 5
status: "empirical"
summary: "Beyond the leading Haar-class scaling, the entropy disagreement exhibits subleading structure that fits numerical data well but for which no analytic derivation yet exists. Included honestly as Open Problem 3."
---

Beyond the leading $d_B^{-3/2}$ scaling of Result 3, the Haar-class disagreement exhibits subleading structure fitted from Monte Carlo at $d_B \in \{16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96\}$ (eq. 5.4):

$$\frac{\mathbb{E}|S_A - S_B|}{(\sqrt{2/\pi}/d_M)\, d_B^{-3/2}} = 1 - \frac{1.13(1)}{d_B} + \frac{4.7(3)}{d_B^2} + O(1/d_B^3)$$

with $\chi^2 = 2.8/4$ dof. The floating-asymptote linear fit $A + B/d_B$ returns $A = 0.994 \pm 0.005$, consistent with the analytic asymptote $A = 1$ at $1.3\sigma$ — a direct statistical test of the prefactor $\sqrt{2/\pi}/d_M$.

We have not analytically derived the subleading coefficients; their origin is non-Gaussian corrections to the Isserlis identity in §5.3. We include this result with a clear statement that it is empirical, not analytic. Listed as an open problem in the paper.
