---

For bulk states drawn Haar-randomly from $\mathcal{H}_A \otimes \mathcal{H}_B \otimes \mathcal{H}_C$ (Theorem 5.2):

$$\mathbb{E}|S_A - S_B| = \sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi}} \cdot \frac{1}{d_M\, d_B^{3/2}}\,(1 + o(1)) \approx \frac{0.798}{d_M} \cdot d_B^{-3/2}$$

The prefactor $\sqrt{2/\pi}/d_M \approx 0.798/d_M$ is exact. No fitting. It arises from the Gaussian-limit identity $\mathbb{E}|X| = \sqrt{2/\pi}\,\sigma_X$ applied to the entropy difference, whose variance scales as $1/(d_B^3 d_M^2)$ (§5.3). Out-of-sample tests at $d_B = 18$ and $d_B = 128$ both pass at sub-$\sigma$ precision. This is the regime relevant to black hole interiors and holographic codes for chaotic quantum dynamics.
