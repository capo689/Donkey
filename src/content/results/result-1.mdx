For any bulk state $|\psi\rangle \in \mathcal{H}_A \otimes \mathcal{H}_B \otimes \mathcal{H}_C$ and a Haar-random non-isometric code $V$, the Haar-averaged observer-$A$ reduced state satisfies the structural identity (Theorem 3.2):

$$\mathbb{E}_V[\rho_{R_A}] = \mathrm{diag}(\rho_A^{\mathrm{bulk}}) + O(1/d^2)$$

where $\rho_A^{\mathrm{bulk}} = \mathrm{Tr}_{BC}(|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|)$ is the bulk $A$-marginal and $\mathrm{diag}(\cdot)$ denotes the diagonal operator in the HUZ cloning basis. The off-diagonal coherences of the bulk marginal are projected out by the $\delta_{aa'}$ structure of the first-moment Haar contraction. This identity reduces the two-observer disagreement problem to a moment calculation of the bulk-marginal diagonal, whose scaling depends on the bulk state class.
